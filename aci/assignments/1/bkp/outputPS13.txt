=================================================================
PEAS Description for the ACO Routing Agent
=================================================================

Performance Measure:
  Minimize total transmission latency (sum of edge weights)
  along the routing path from source to destination router.

Environment:
  A computer communication network modeled as a weighted
  undirected graph. Nodes represent routers, edges represent
  communication links with associated latency costs.
  The environment is fully observable, static, discrete,
  and deterministic.

Actuators:
  - Selection of the next router (hop) in the path
  - Deposition of pheromone on traversed edges
  - Pheromone evaporation mechanism

Sensors:
  - Current pheromone levels on all edges (tau)
  - Edge latency values for heuristic computation (eta = 1/latency)
  - Set of visited/unvisited nodes to avoid cycles
  - Identity of current node and destination node

=================================================================
Case 1
Nodes (Routers): 0, 1, 2, 3, 4
Source: 0, Destination: 4
=================================================================

--- Dijkstra's Algorithm (Optimal Shortest Path) ---
Best Path: 0 -> 4
Minimum Latency: 6

--- ACO Scenario 1 ---
Parameters: alpha=1.0, beta=2.0, rho=0.5, ants=10, iterations=100
Best Path: 0 -> 1 -> 2 -> 4
Minimum Latency: 6
Converged at Iteration: 1
Matches Dijkstra's Optimal: Yes

--- ACO Scenario 2 ---
Parameters: alpha=2.5, beta=1.0, rho=0.3, ants=10, iterations=100
Best Path: 0 -> 1 -> 2 -> 4
Minimum Latency: 6
Converged at Iteration: 1
Matches Dijkstra's Optimal: Yes

--- Comparison Analysis ---

1. Convergence Speed:
   Scenario 1 converged at iteration: 1
   Scenario 2 converged at iteration: 1
   -> Both scenarios converged at the same iteration.

2. Comparison with Dijkstra's Optimal Path:
   Dijkstra's optimal cost: 6
   Scenario 1 ACO cost: 6 (Deviation: 0)
   Scenario 2 ACO cost: 6 (Deviation: 0)
   -> Both ACO scenarios successfully found the optimal path.

3. Effect of Parameters (alpha, beta, rho) on Solution Quality:

   Scenario 1 (alpha=1.0, beta=2.0, rho=0.5):
     - Higher beta (2.0) places more emphasis on the heuristic
       (inverse latency), guiding ants toward shorter edges.
     - Moderate alpha (1.0) balances pheromone influence.
     - Higher evaporation rate (rho=0.5) promotes exploration by
       reducing old pheromone faster, preventing premature convergence.

   Scenario 2 (alpha=2.5, beta=1.0, rho=0.3):
     - Higher alpha (2.5) heavily favors pheromone-rich paths,
       leading to stronger exploitation of discovered routes.
     - Lower beta (1.0) reduces the influence of edge latency
       heuristic, making path selection more pheromone-dependent.
     - Lower evaporation rate (rho=0.3) retains pheromone longer,
       reinforcing previously found paths but risking premature
       convergence to suboptimal solutions.
